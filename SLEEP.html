SLEEP WELL, LIVE WELL

Sleep is one of the most common activities in our daily basis. Consequently, it’s important to understand how it works and what further implications it can have in live.

Sleep may be defined in many different ways, such as: a state of rest, a dynamical and physiological behavior, a homeostatic response, among others. That's why, as it involves so many processes in the human body, sleep’s function is still not merely defined. Nevertheless, for this same reason, knowing sleep takes place in all stages of our lives, let’s go through it and understand how our brain respond when we fall asleep.

Sleep is described by three principal factors: eye movement, muscle contraction and brain activity, which define each stage of sleep. For this reason, the study by which sleep may be analyzed is a polysomnography, that is composed by an electrooculography (EOG), an electromyography (EMG) and an electroencephalography (ECG). Sleep has two stages known as no Rem sleep (NRem) and Rem sleep (Rem). These stages are described by brain waves exposed in the ECG such as: Alpha, Beta, Theta and Delta, which differ in amplitude and frequency. From wakefulness people enter NRem phase as sleep gets deeper, which diminishes all eye and muscle movement. Once the person is absolutely asleep, Rem phase begins and brain and ocular activity become similar as of someone that’s awake. In contrary, in this phase, muscle activity is minimal and EMG amplitude drops to its lowest level. These explains why Rem stands for Rapid Eye Movement and occurs when dreams take place. Sleep changes during the night by a lot of physiological factors. However, there are some regions of the brain that activate or disactivate in each sleep stage. For instance, during Rem sleep the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex and the posterior cingulate cortex are inactivated; while the anterior cingulate cortex, the amygdala and the parahippocampal gyrus are activated. Because sleep has influence in so many aspects of human live, a unified function hasn’t been defined. However, there are some scientific speculations, for example: contribution to the immunological system, savings of energy, neural connections consolidation and enhance cognitive performance, among others.
