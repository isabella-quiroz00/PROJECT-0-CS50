COVID-19 PANDEMIC

Coronavirus disease is an infectious disease caused by a respiratory virus. Nowadays, the rate of transmission of the virus has augmented and the OMS declared it a pandemic. Also, although COVID-19 affects differently all patients, thereÂ´s evidence of specific symptoms presented in most infected patients. Some of the most common ones are fever, dry cough, tiredness and respiratory difficulties or shortness. However, far beyond those visible symptoms, scientists discovered that patients with Coronavirus have presented neurological symptoms as well.

GENERAL INFORMATION
1. People can get infected principally by the respiratory airway.
2. Most common symptoms start showing up two weeks after getting infected with the virus.
3. 36.4% of Coronavirus patients presented some neurological diagnoses.
4. 15% of the people infected manifested deficiency or alterations of consciousness.
NERVOUS SYSTEM
The hematogenous route, is the principal way of virus invasion of the nervous system.
The virus enters the respiratory system and travels all the way down to the lungs. Once there, during gas exchange in the alveoli, the virus gets to the blood.
Consequently, throughout the blood it crosses the blood brain barrier and have direct access to the nervous system.

CENTRAL NERVOUS SYSTEM
The principal neurological symptoms are:
1. Dizziness
2. Muscular pain
3. Strong headaches
4. Stroke
PERIPHERAL NERVOUS SYSTEM
The principal neurological symptoms are:
1. Loss of smell
2. Decreased sense of touch and taste
3. Decreased balance and coordination
4. Pain for harm in nerves
